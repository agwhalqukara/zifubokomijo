Russell Simmons Biography

Russell Wendell Simmons was born on October 4, 1957, in Queens, New York, to parents Daniel Simmons and Evelyn Simons. He was raised in Queens. Rev Run and Daniel Simmons Jr. are his brothers.

In 1975, he graduated from August Martin High School. Simmons then attended City College of New York in Harlem. He met Kurt Walker there, who advised him to join the hip-hop phenomenon.

Russell Simmons married Kimora Lee in December 1998. Ming Lee and Aoki Lee, the couple’s two daughters, were born in 2000 and 2002, respectively. In 2006, they divorced. He is a Christian Yogi who respects all religions. He practices Transcendental Meditation and is a vegan and Ahimsa advocate.

Simmons was accused of sexual assault by Keri Claussen Khalighi in 1991 in November 2017. Many other women, including Amanda Seales, Kelly Cutrone, Lisa Kirk, Natasha Williams-Blach, and Toni Sallie, have accused him of sexual abuse. Jennifer Jarosik, Drew Dixon, Tina Baker, Sherri Hines, and Jenny Lumet have also charged him with rape.

Career

Russell Simmons, along with Rick Rubin, founded Def Jam Records in 1983. He co-produced and starred in the film ‘Krush Groove two years later. He has also co-produced the HBO shows Def Poetry Jam’ and ‘Def Comedy Jam.’

He worked with Def Jam to release music for artists such as LL Cool J, Beastie Boys, and others. The music label was later purchased by Universal Music Group for $100 million and renamed The Island Def Jam Music Group.

He was a co-producer on the commercially successful 1996 film ‘The Nutty Professor.’ Simmons produced the film ‘Dude, Where’s My Car?’ in 2000 and ‘It’s a Funny Story in 2010.

In 1999, he began working for the company Rush Communications. He is the current CEO of Rush Communications. Simmons is also a published author, having written books such as ‘Life and Def: Sex, Drugs, Money, and God and ‘Success Through Stillness.’

Russell Simmons Achievements and Awards

Russell Simmons was nominated for a CableACE for Def Comedy Jam in 1993. At the GLAAD Media Awards in 2011, he received the Excellence in Media Award. He also received the Vanguard Award at the 2009 Image Awards. Simmons received the Visionary Award at the PGA Awards in 2013. In 2014, he was awarded an Honorary Doctorate Degree in Humane Letters by Claremont Lincoln University.
